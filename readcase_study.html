About Siemens

Siemens is a technology pioneer and global powerhouse. It transforms businesses and industries to improve the lives of billions of people around the world.

The company delivers purpose-driven digitalization, automation, and engineering for more efficient manufacturing, smarter power distribution, stronger supply chains, and more.

With 293,000 employees worldwide, Siemens is a leading player in industry 4.0. It’s at the cutting edge of infrastructure, transport, and healthcare.

The Challenge

Siemens uses technology to overcome real-world challenges. As a digital pioneer, its marketing strategy needs to align with the company’s mission and digital-first branding. It has to meet customers where they are on digital channels.

“One of the goals of the company is to help our customers in their digital transformation,” says Guenter Gaugler, Head of Content Production at Siemens, “Lumen5 helps us in getting more digital in our communications - that’s part of the digital transformation.”

“The times of total control are gone. Today, you have to be responsive. You have to empower your communicators to do the right things themselves and not go through all the approval processes that we used to have in the past.”

“We are trying to empower our people to be able to do the modern way of communication,” adds Guenter. “Next year, Siemens will turn 175 years old, and that is only possible because the company has been transforming all the time.”

Keeping Messaging and Branding Consistent Across a Global Organization

The Siemens brand covers multiple departments and teams in different countries. So maintaining consistent branding and messaging across all digital channels is a challenge.

The modern buyer’s journey is increasingly fragmented and spread over a growing number of channels. The average buyer will have 6-8 interactions with your brand before becoming a viable sales lead, making brand consistency more important than ever.

With the drive to expand its digital presence, Siemens risked messaging becoming disjointed and diluting its brand image.

Lumen5 helps Siemens overcome this challenge by developing custom designs and templates exclusive to the Siemens brand. As it expands and develops its digital communications, the Lumen5 product is continually updated to meet Siemens' needs.

Customer success manager, Izzy, acted as liaison between the Siemens Branding and Creative Production teams and the Lumen5 Template team. She works closely with Siemens to produce the custom templates and communicate the updates to end-users.

Siemens maintains control over its content so that every video published tells a consistent brand story. “The onboarding process was smooth and effective!” says Michelle, “the Lumen5 team was a great help to set everything up.”

Lumen5’s customer success manager also takes care of account management. So, the Siemens team can focus on content creation, not onboarding new users and admin.

“Izzy’s position has really helped to get things running smoothly,” says Michelle, “basically, shifting everything from an account management part to Izzy.”

Regular meetings and reviews with the Siemens team ensure that goals are met, and strategy is always aligned. Siemens can see account analytics, including the number of videos created by users, creation time, adoption levels, and more.

Lumen5 is a strategic partner in Siemens’ digital transformation journey. Feedback from the Siemens team is constantly fed into product development and updates to improve the experience for end-users.
